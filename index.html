Altamira, Tamaulipas

The city of Altamira was founded on May 2, 1749, in honor of the viceregal official Juan Rodríguez de Albuerne, Marqués de Altamira, by Don José de Escandón y Helguera, Conde de la Sierra Gorda, under the patronage of Our Lady of Las Caldas. The name "Huasteca" derives from the Nahuatl word "Huaxteco" or "Cuexteco," which, according to Father Sahagún, comes from a leader named Cuextecatl. The Mexicans referred to the region as "Huaxtecapan," but it was also called "Pantlan" or "Panotlan," and later "Pánuco," which means "the place through which they pass."
